For a design challenge, I created Treet, an environmental goals tracker. I first worked on the initial problem and first iteration. I later went back to the project to work on research processes and adding new interface ideas to the app.

   View the first prototype

The problem


A small yet impactful issue in the environment space involves people not changing their daily habits to reflect a more eco-friendly identity. Such things include showering for a shorter amount of time, sorting between trash and recycling, carpooling to work, etc. People don't feel like these small actions make a difference in the grander schemes of things, and thus, feel less inclined to change their habits to make that difference.

The solution


Treet works by combining the typical daily goals tracker with environmental tidbits that can help reduce someone's carbon footprint. I sought to think of some main reasons someone puts off being more eco-friendly and tried to offer solutions within the app:

Why is it hard to find ways to be eco-friendly? The app offers an explore section with different popular goals among users, trending goals that users are recently testing out, and a "just for you" area that is tailored based on how often a user commits to a goal.

Are users looking to have a fun experience? We incorporated gradients to highlight our light-hearted ethos. Users are allowed to assign gradients to different goals, and create their own.

How can we encourage people to share this with friends? Having a notes section within each goal makes the task seem more personal. Users can make notes if they are working with a friend to complete a task, and spread the news through word-of-mouth.
